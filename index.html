Know when the bottle was opened.

TwistLog is a reusable smart ring for prescription bottles. It tracks cap-opening events, helps surface possible double-dose moments, and supports reminder routines without asking you to manually log every dose.

The daily gap

Most reminders still depend on memory.

Pill organizers, phone alarms, and app checklists can all be useful. But they still ask a person to prepare, remember, dismiss, or self-report. TwistLog starts from the physical action that already happens: opening the bottle.

The moment TwistLog is built around Someone looks at a bottle and genuinely cannot remember whether it was already opened that morning.

No new container routine

The ring is designed to work around existing prescription bottles instead of replacing the entire setup with a countertop dispenser.

Less manual self-reporting

The app can still offer fallback logging, but the primary signal is the twist/opening event from the bottle hardware.

Per-bottle history

Each ring can be assigned to a specific bottle, making the log more useful than a generic reminder being dismissed.

How it works

A simple loop for a high-stress moment.

TwistLog is being designed as a hardware-plus-app system: ring events become clean app logs, and the app turns those logs into useful reminders and history.

1

Snap on the ring

Place TwistLog around a compatible prescription bottle neck and assign it to that medication in the app.

2

Detect the twist

The sensor watches for the bottle-opening motion and sends the event to the companion app.

3

Log the opening

The app stores the event time so you can check whether the bottle was opened during the expected window.

4

Nudge when needed

If a scheduled bottle has not been opened, TwistLog can support iPhone and Apple Watch reminders.

Positioning

Between a reminder app and a full dispenser.

The goal is not to replace clinical care or pharmacy systems. The goal is a practical household tool that gives people a better reference point than memory alone.

Reminder apps

  • Easy to install
  • Require manual confirmation
  • Can be dismissed without bottle activity

TwistLog

  • Tracks bottle-opening events
  • Works with existing routines
  • Designed for per-bottle history and reminders

Automatic dispensers

  • Powerful for some care needs
  • Bulky and expensive
  • Ask users to transfer pills into a new system
Built carefully

Clear language. Clear limits.

TwistLog is being built as a reminder and bottle-opening log, not as a medical device. That distinction should be visible in the product, the app, and the website from day one.

Opening, not ingestion

TwistLog tracks when a bottle appears to be opened. It does not verify that medicine was swallowed.

Reference, not advice

The log is for personal reference and reminders. It is not a substitute for medical advice or professional care.

Privacy-minded

The product should make medication names, logs, and sharing choices explicit before launch.
