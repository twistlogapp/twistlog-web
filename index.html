The daily gap

Most reminders still depend on memory.

Pill organizers, phone alarms, and app checklists can all be useful. But they still ask a person to prepare, remember, dismiss, or self-report. TwistLog starts with a simple bottle-opening history that can work before hardware automation is ready.

The moment TwistLog is built around Someone looks at a bottle and genuinely cannot remember whether it was already opened that morning.

No new container routine

The app is designed around the prescription bottles people already use instead of requiring a countertop dispenser or pill transfer.

Fast manual logging first

The first version can focus on a quick "Opened now" action, recent history, and reminders while hardware detection is tested.

Per-bottle history

Each tracked bottle can have its own reminder routine and opening history, making the log more useful than a generic alarm being dismissed.

How it works

A simple loop for a high-stress moment.

TwistLog starts as a simple app loop: log the opening, check history, and use reminders when routines get fuzzy. Prototype hardware can later make the opening log automatic.

Positioning

Between a reminder app and a full dispenser.

The goal is not to replace clinical care or pharmacy systems. The goal is a practical household tool that gives people a better reference point than memory alone.

Reminder apps

  • Easy to install
  • Require manual confirmation
  • Can be dismissed without bottle activity

TwistLog

  • Quick bottle-opening log
  • Works with existing bottles and routines
  • Optional ring detection in prototype

Automatic dispensers

  • Powerful for some care needs
  • Bulky and expensive
  • Ask users to transfer pills into a new system
Built carefully

Clear language. Clear limits.

TwistLog is being built as a reminder and bottle-opening log, not as a medical device. That distinction should be visible in the product, the app, and the website from day one.

Opening, not ingestion

TwistLog tracks when a bottle appears to be opened. It does not verify that medicine was swallowed.

Reference, not advice

The log is for personal reference and reminders. It is not a substitute for medical advice or professional care.

Privacy-minded

The product should make medication names, logs, and sharing choices explicit before launch.
